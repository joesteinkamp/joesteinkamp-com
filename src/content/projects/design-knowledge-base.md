Design Knowledge Base is an open-source design-documentation repository — a single,
structured place to learn how product design actually gets done. It's the resource I
kept wishing existed: not a link dump, but an organized body of knowledge that maps the
whole discipline.

## The structure

The Base is organized into five top-level pillars, each going from first principles to
day-to-day practice:

- **Framework** — the methodologies and playbooks behind a design process, including a
  process model that moves through *Explore → Decide → Emerge*.
- **Craft** — the hands-on skills and execution that make work good.
- **Mindset** — the thinking and philosophy that shapes how designers approach problems.
- **Operations** — how design runs at scale: consistency, cohesion, and reusable
  solutions to recurring problems.
- **Leadership** — growing people and leading design teams.

## Built in the open

Everything lives in a public repository and is free to read, fork, and contribute to.
The goal is a shared, evolving reference for the craft community rather than a static
personal blog — design education that improves as more people add to it.

It's also paired with [companion screenshot galleries](https://screenshots.designknowledgebase.com/)
documenting the best-designed products on the web, used throughout the Base as concrete
examples of the principles in practice.
