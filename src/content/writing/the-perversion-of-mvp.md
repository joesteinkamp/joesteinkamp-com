Somewhere along the way "MVP" stopped meaning *the smallest thing that delivers
real value* and started meaning *the smallest thing we can get away with shipping*.
The word that did the damage is **viable**. Teams optimize the "minimum" and treat
"viable" as negotiable.

A viable product earns the right to exist. It does one thing well enough that a
person would choose it on purpose. That bar is higher than "it compiles and the
happy path works," and it's the whole point.
